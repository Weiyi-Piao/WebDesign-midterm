The Art of Abstraction

The technique of Abstraction is ubiquitous.
For example, if you asked for directions, they might tell you something like:

  1. Go down this road, until you see an KFC
  2. Then, turn right and walked until the end of the street
  3. Now you can see an old yellow building.
  4. The place you are finding is on the other side of that road

Now you may understand how to go to that place, but do you know things below?

  1. How far is here to the KFC?
  2. Which angle should I do to
  3. Where is the yellow building?
  4. Which direction is the yellowing building facing?

Well, you are not necessary to know about it, but these informations are very important for the computer to run the program.

That's how abstraction works:
You can use something that you are don't understand.



Advantages & Disadvantages

Firstly, you don't need to understand how does those functions work.
Secondly, it makes the program more readable.
Moreover, a function with objects can make the code more readable.


However, because the details are hidden from the user, we can't improve things inside those modules, functions, or objects.
